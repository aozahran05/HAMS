Business Case

1. Introduction/Background

Healthcare services are essential for maintaining individuals' health and treating illnesses, and they require a high level of accuracy and reliability. However, manual appointment scheduling often results in errors, inefficiencies, and patient dissatisfaction. The Healthcare Appointment Management System (HAMS) is designed to automate and streamline the appointment scheduling process, ensuring greater efficiency, accuracy, and improved patient satisfaction.

2. Business Objectives

  • To implement an automated appointment scheduling system.
  • To enhance efficiency in managing healthcare staff and patients.
  • To protect patient privacy and minimize scheduling errors.
  • To improve monitoring, reporting, and performance evaluation.

3. Current Situation and Problem / Opportunity Statement

Current Problems: Manual work makes mistakes and gives bad experience for patients.

  • Appointments are written and managed by hand.
  • This causes missing information, double booking, and wasted time.
  • Patients feel their privacy is not safe.

Opportunity: The opportunity is to use HAMS to make appointments automatic, faster, and more accurately.

4. Critical Assumptions and Constraints

Assumptions

Staff Adaptation: Staff and patients will adapt to the new system.
IT Infrastructure: Necessary IT infrastructure is available.

Constraints

Internet Dependency: Dependence on reliable internet connectivity.
User Resistance: People find it hard to communicate with machines instead of staff.
Resources: Limited budget and time availability.
